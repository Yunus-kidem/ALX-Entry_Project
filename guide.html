Correct way to approach programming

This ALX application taught me so much & was a real eye opener. I have been watching tutorials & courses the past 4 month but I didn’t internalize the concepts until now. What I realized is that I have to get my hands dirty & start actually working on a project. Tutorials are going to help you but they’re not going to help you make the things you’re going to make.

Right way to learn

I challenge you to start a project & as you are building the project, if you get stuck then you can go back & look up those things as soon as you learn, you come back & apply it.

That’s how each concept is going to be internalized, is going to be beyond just this superficial level of understanding.

Especially...

If you want to become a self-taught developer, the terrible way of doing it is just hoping, you keep learning & learning, and then one day you dream you’re going to be ready for something, right!

Let me ask you one question? --- how many projects have you built so far?

If the answer is very few, less than 3-5 of your own projects then there’s a reason:

  • you are not improving at level you want to improve
  • You’re stuck, where you’re stuck
  • You’re not earning from coding
Learning as you go will allow you to grow at a rapid pace...

Now if you start building your own projects & you learn as you go, taking away that fear of the unknown, stop waiting just to learn all of it. Now you’re going to be in a really amazing position my friend:

  • You’re going to be learning faster than you’ve ever have
  • You will internalize all the information
  • You will be able to apply it to your projects

…and that way you’re not wasting time.
